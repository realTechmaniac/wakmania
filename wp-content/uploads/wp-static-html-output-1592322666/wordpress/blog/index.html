Hurray it is my Dad's birthday

Today is my fathers birthday. He is 72 “official age ” lol.

I think of people without fathers and I always wonder how would it have turned out for me if my father wasn’t there

I remember growing up with a very strict dad who when I was younger sometimes it led to resentment. I would say he’s always angry but as an older man I have realised the pressure fathers face to do good for their children

Fear of the unknown, how will I put food on the table for my kids, how can I give them the best. You have all these plans then “life hits you. In my fathers case that’s exactly what happened. He went from an aspiring diplomat to not having an income

Growing up with a silver spoon to things got worse where we basically had nothing at a certain period ,which is what happens to a lot of African fathers with a volatile economy .

As a privileged kid all I could see was the anger of ” why are things so difficult”, “why can’t I have the best sneakers.” And often blamed him for not doing better, not looking at the sacrifices he made for us.

I did not know the immense pressure my father was in. The sleepless nights he had at the time.

I want to thank my father for the man I have become and all fathers who work effortlessly to bring their kids up to be great products of society.

It is easy to be a dad but very difficult to be a father.

Happy birthday KAJ. Love you very much

Welcome to my World

I was told every thumb is a finger but not every finger is a thumb, in order to find yourself is to accept your mistakes, accept that it’s on you to be your best and blame nobody for your outcomes but you.

Yes we are all fingers on earth, but how many stand out like the thumb. How many of us are truly pushing to find ourselves, fighting for what we truly believe in, pushing our goals.
So last year was one of the hardest years I had to deal with. Didn’t talk much about what I was dealing with but felt compelled to write today.

I had it all planned the year before. Save up, pay some debts and settle down eventually.
As the saying goes, life has a way of humbling people. You plan then life happens
But I also learned God sometimes tests you to see what you are truly capable of.
Also, when he wants to use you, he clears all the dead weight around you and only if you are ready to go through the journey, only then will you find your true strength.

For 5 years apart from my normal 9 to 5, I have been working on my goals. I love to connect and empower and I have done so consistently.
I am trying to be the thumb that leads the finger and it can be a very doubtful, lonely and a dark road but in going through the dark road you find a lot about the strength you had. I know im not going to be an older me *living a life of regrets*.

Keep trying to be the thumb and even though it doesn’t make sense now or you are highly doubtful, you will always find your strength within.

Forget about fitting in and keep pushing yourself to be mentally better.

Keep grinding
